# Decision Gate - 2026-05-14

## Decision

Pivot the 2026 half-marathon Goal A race from 2026-10-04 to 2026-12-06, keep the Hanson-inspired 18-week block structure, and use the added time to extend the base.

## Facts

- The long-term goal is a Boston Marathon qualifying attempt over roughly 3 years.
- The practical marathon planning target is about 2:55:00 or faster.
- The previous race target was the 2026-10-04 half marathon.
- The new race target is the 2026-12-06 half marathon.
- The shifted 18-week block begins 2026-08-03.
- The known limiter is consistency and discontinuity after gaps.

## Preference

Use the extra runway to build durability first. Keep phase 1 of the base at 5 days/week, then progress toward 6 days/week only if consistency and recovery clearly support it.

## Risk

The main risk is turning the added calendar space into premature mileage chasing. The secondary risk is carrying too much fatigue into the formal half-marathon block.

## Adaptation

Keep the existing Hanson-inspired plan structure and workout logic, but move the dates forward. Extend the pre-block ramp through 2026-08-02. Use the added base to normalize frequency and moderate volume rather than force early peak mileage. Treat 30-35 miles/week by block start as the minimum good outcome and 35-40 miles/week as the better outcome if earned.

## Final Call

Proceed with the December 6 pivot. This preserves the current framework, extends the base, and gives the fall block a better chance of reaching 50-55 miles/week in a stable way.

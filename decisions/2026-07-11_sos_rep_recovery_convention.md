# Decision Gate - 2026-07-11

## Decision

Define a standard rep-recovery convention (jog length, time, and pace) for all
SOS sessions in the 2026 half-marathon block, recorded once in the plan
framework rather than per week.

## Facts

- The 18-week week files specify SOS reps but never the recovery between them.
- Recoveries apply identically across all weeks by rep type, not by date.
- The block uses four rep families: speed, strength, threshold, and HMP, plus
  strides.

## Preference

Keep Hanson-style short recoveries for strength/threshold/HMP reps and honest
easy-pace recovery jogs throughout, expressed as ranges rather than fixed
numbers so they adapt to track vs road and daily fatigue.

## Risk

The main risk is recoveries drifting long enough that SOS days become interval
sessions with full rest, losing the cumulative-fatigue emphasis. The convention
counts recovery-jog mileage inside each week's total, so it does not change any
mileage target.

## Adaptation

Recovery jogs are run at recovery/easy effort (~8:45-9:30/mi). Speed reps get
400-600m jogs; strength reps 400-800m; threshold and HMP blocks 400-800m;
strides get full walk/stand recovery. Continuous and progression runs have no
interval recovery.

## Final Call

Adopt the Rep Recovery Guide in `plans/2026-half-marathon/03_framework.md` as
the single source of truth for SOS recoveries. Revisit only if reps repeatedly
collapse rather than drift, in which case apply the Adjustment Rules before
lengthening recoveries.

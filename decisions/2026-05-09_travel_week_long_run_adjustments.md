# Decision Gate - 2026-05-09

## Decision

Adjust the final two pre-block ramp weeks around cruise travel instead of forcing the original Sunday long-run structure.

## Facts

- The 18-week half-marathon block starts on 2026-06-01.
- The week of 2026-05-18 includes Sunday embarkation.
- The week of 2026-05-25 includes cruise-ship logistics and a Sunday flight.
- The pre-block ramp is base building only.
- The current limiter is consistency and training discontinuity, not a need to maximize pre-block mileage.

## Preference

Preserve running rhythm and enter the block healthy. Keep easy volume distributed across the week, but allow travel days to shape where the long run lands.

## Risk

The main risk is treating travel weeks as mileage tests and trying to recover missed miles in awkward windows. That could turn easy base building into fatigue before the formal block starts.

## Adaptation

- For the week of 2026-05-18, move the long run to Saturday and make Sunday embarkation day flexible.
- For the week of 2026-05-25, keep Sunday fully off for flight day and use Saturday as a shortened long-ish run.
- Keep all running easy, with strides optional only when the setup is safe and convenient.
- Do not force makeup mileage if cruise logistics shorten a run.

## Final Call

Use travel-aware weeks as the default plan. The goal is to arrive at 2026-06-01 consistent, fresh, and ready to start the Hanson-inspired block, even if the final ramp week lands below the original 30-32 mile target.

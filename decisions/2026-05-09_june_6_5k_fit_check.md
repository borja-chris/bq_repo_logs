# Six-Hat Decision - 2026-05-09

## White Hat - Facts and Constraints

- The 5K is scheduled for 2026-06-06.
- The 18-week half-marathon block starts 2026-06-01.
- The Goal A race remains the 2026-10-04 half marathon.
- The current framework is Hanson-inspired half-marathon training.
- The known limiter is consistency and training discontinuity after gaps.
- A 5K is useful for current-fitness feedback, but it is not the same as half-marathon readiness.

## Red Hat - Preference and Motivation

Use the 5K as a clean early fit check. The race should sharpen intent and expose whether early training paces are reasonable without becoming a referendum on the whole cycle.

## Black Hat - Risk Control

The main risk is overreacting to the result. A strong race should not trigger an automatic move toward 58-60 miles/week, and a poor race should not downgrade the half-marathon cycle by itself. The other risk is carrying 5K soreness into week 2 and forcing normal SOS work too soon.

## Yellow Hat - Upside

The 5K can help set early threshold and stamina paces, show whether the pre-block ramp has taken, and provide a before/after marker for the October half-marathon build.

## Green Hat - Adaptation and Creativity

Modify week 1 so the 5K is the primary quality session. Replace normal Tuesday/Thursday SOS work with easy running, relaxed strides, and pre-race recovery. Resume normal Hanson structure in week 2 only if the race and recovery are normal.

## Blue Hat - Final Decision

Race the 2026-06-06 5K hard as a fit check, recover from it, and make only conservative changes afterward. The 5K gets one vote; the next 7-10 days of consistency and recovery get the deciding vote.

## Goal Bands

- Goal A: strong fit check. Race evenly or slightly negative split, recover normally within 3-4 days, and use the result to support confidence in early threshold and stamina work. A rough time marker is sub-22:00 if current fitness is ahead of the baseline.
- Goal B: useful baseline. Race honestly, hold form, and avoid a major late collapse. A rough time marker is 22:00-23:30.
- Goal C: complete the diagnostic without damage. Finish without forcing if the day goes sideways, then resume training normally. A rough time marker is 23:30-25:00+, or any result where weather, pacing, or soreness clearly limits performance.

## Post-Race Adjustment Rules

- If the race is strong and recovery is quick, adjust workout paces slightly faster but keep the mileage plan basically unchanged.
- If the race is okay but fades late, prioritize threshold control and aerobic consistency over faster intervals.
- If the race is poor or leaves abnormal soreness, hold the early block near the low end and protect consistency before adding load.

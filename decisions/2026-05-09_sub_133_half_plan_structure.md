# Decision Gate - 2026-05-09

## Decision

Use a sub-1:33 checkpoint target for the 2026-10-04 half marathon and build an 18-week Hanson-inspired plan from an assumed 30-mile weekly base.

## Facts

- The long-term goal is a Boston Marathon qualifying attempt over roughly 3 years.
- The practical marathon planning target is about 2:55:00 or faster.
- The current race target is the 2026-10-04 half marathon.
- The 18-week block begins 2026-06-01.
- The assumed starting base is about 30 miles/week.
- The known limiter is consistency and discontinuity after gaps.

## Preference

Use Hanson-inspired structure: frequent running, cumulative fatigue, moderate long runs, two SOS days, a Wednesday recovery break, and a strong emphasis on threshold, stamina, and half-marathon-pace work.

## Risk

The main risk is turning the sub-1:33 checkpoint into a forced pace target before the runner has earned the load. The secondary risk is chasing peak mileage at the expense of consistency and recovery.

## Adaptation

The default plan uses six running days, Wednesday as the off day, Tuesday and Thursday SOS workouts, and Sunday long runs mostly in the 9-15 mile range. Monday, Wednesday, and Friday are treated as flexible recovery/easy slots when scheduling requires a swap. Peak mileage is about 54 miles/week by default, with no automatic move to 58-60 miles/week.

## Final Call

Proceed with the sub-1:33 checkpoint as an ambitious but conditional target. The cycle is successful if it produces consistent training, healthy durability, and a strong half-marathon performance without compromising the longer BQ arc.
